Daily Care — For All Mandirs

Two minutes a day prevents almost every problem

🌅 Before Puja
  • Dust gently with a clean, soft, dry cotton cloth — shelves, murtis, carvings, base
  • Check that yesterday's flowers, prasad, and diya residue have been cleared
🪔 During Puja
  • Always place a steel or silver thali under diyas, incense, and any vessel with ghee or water
  • Use drip-proof diya plates or deeper oil vessels
  • Keep a dedicated dry cloth inside the mandir — not used elsewhere
  • Never place a hot camphor plate or burning diya directly on any surface — use a metal coaster
🌙 After Puja
  • Remove flowers and leftover prasad — sugar and moisture damage every material
  • Wipe any visible drips of ghee, oil, milk, or water with a dry cloth first
  • Let any damp surface air-dry fully before replacing items
Home Practice: Keep a small stack of cut cotton cloths (old soft cotton kurtas work beautifully) in a drawer near the mandir. Use a fresh piece every week.

Care by Material

Click your material to expand the full care guide

🪨

Marble

Natural stone — timeless, porous, acid-sensitive
▼

Cleaning Routine

  • Daily: dust with dry soft cotton cloth
  • Weekly: damp wipe with plain water on a well-wrung cloth, then dry immediately
  • Monthly: gently brush carvings with a soft dry toothbrush, then damp-wipe
  • Occasional: a few drops of pH-neutral soap (Pril, baby shampoo) in warm water — always rinse with plain water afterwards

Critical Warnings

  • Never use lemon, vinegar, or any acidic cleaner — they etch marble permanently
  • Never use Harpic, Lizol, Colin, or bathroom/floor cleaners
  • Never use scouring pads, steel wool, or hard brushes
  • Never leave water, ghee, oil, or flowers sitting overnight — marble is porous and will absorb stains

Common Stains

  • Ghee/oil: blot immediately, sprinkle talcum powder or multani mitti generously, leave 24 hours, brush off, wipe
  • Haldi: blot, apply baking soda + water paste, wait 10 minutes, wipe
  • Kumkum, milk, wax: see Stain Guide section below
Signs it needs professional care: Dull patches after acidic spill, visible cracks, chips on prominent edges — call us for polish or repair.
⬜

Solid Surface (Corian)

Non-porous composite — modern, seamless, forgiving
▼

Cleaning Routine

  • Daily: wipe with a damp soft cloth or microfibre
  • Weekly: warm water with a few drops of mild dish soap, then wipe with damp cloth and dry
  • Most stains come off easily — no special techniques needed
  • Matte finish: a melamine foam eraser (Magic Eraser) can be used gently on stubborn marks

Critical Warnings

  • Never place very hot items directly on the surface — use a metal or ceramic coaster
  • Avoid acetone, nail polish remover, paint thinner, or strong solvents
  • Avoid harsh abrasives on gloss finishes — they can leave fine scratches
  • Do not cut anything directly on the mandir surface

Minor Scratches

  • Matte finish: buff out light scratches with a fine Scotch-Brite pad in a circular motion, then wipe clean
  • Gloss finish: do not attempt — call us for professional buffing
🪵

Solid Wood

Natural — warm, living, sensitive to water and sun
▼

Cleaning Routine

  • Daily: dust with a clean dry soft cloth only
  • Weekly: wipe with a barely damp cotton cloth (wring until it feels almost dry), then immediately go over with a dry cloth
  • Never allow water to sit on wood — even for a few minutes
  • Every 6 months: apply a thin coat of teak oil or beeswax-based polish with a soft cloth, buff to a shine

Critical Warnings

  • Never use water-heavy cleaning — wood warps, swells, and cracks with moisture
  • Never place wet vessels, lotas, or flower bowls directly on wood — water rings are permanent
  • Never place hot items on wood — finish will bubble or discolour
  • Keep away from direct sunlight — wood fades and the finish yellows over years
  • Control humidity — avoid placing near AC vents, heaters, or damp walls
Signs it needs professional care: Crack or split in the wood, finish peeling or bubbling, deep water damage — call us. We can often re-polish or patch invisibly.
✨

HDHMR + DUCO

Engineered board with high-gloss automotive PU paint
▼

Cleaning Routine

  • Daily: wipe with a clean dry microfibre cloth — this finish shows every fingerprint
  • Weekly: very lightly damp microfibre with plain water, then dry-buff with a clean microfibre
  • Smudges: a drop of mild soap in water, applied with microfibre, then wiped clean
  • Every 3-6 months: automotive-grade gloss polish (3M or Meguiar's) keeps the shine deep

Critical Warnings

  • NEVER use acetone, thinner, nail polish remover — DUCO paint will dissolve or discolour
  • Never use abrasives — any scouring pad will leave visible fine scratches on the gloss
  • Never use ammonia-based cleaners (Colin) — they dull the finish
  • No hot diyas or camphor plates directly on DUCO — heat causes white marks very difficult to remove
Signs it needs professional care: Visible scratches, heat marks, chipped paint — DUCO can be professionally re-sprayed. Do not attempt touch-up paints yourself.
💎

Acrylic + Prelam

Pre-laminated board with glossy acrylic top layer
▼

Cleaning Routine

  • Daily: wipe with a soft damp microfibre — never dry-wipe acrylic, dry dust particles scratch the surface
  • Always wet the cloth first, even for a quick wipe
  • Weekly: a few drops of mild dish soap in warm water, then wipe with clean damp cloth, then dry-buff

Critical Warnings

  • NEVER dry-wipe — dust acts like sandpaper on acrylic and leaves fine scratches
  • Never use ammonia-based cleaners (Colin), alcohol-based cleaners, or glass cleaners — they cloud and craze acrylic
  • Keep away from direct heat — hot diyas, camphor, or heaters can warp acrylic
  • Prelam edges are sensitive to water seeping in — always dry edges carefully after cleaning
Signs it needs professional care: Clouding, fine scratch network, bubbling at the acrylic edge, prelam edge peeling or swelling — call us.

Do's & Don'ts

Keep this visible near your mandir. When in doubt, refer to it.

✓ Always Do

  • Wipe spills immediately
  • Use coasters under diyas and hot items
  • Dust / wipe daily as per your material
  • Use pH-neutral mild soap when needed
  • Keep mandir away from direct sunlight
  • Let wet surfaces air-dry fully
  • Handle with two hands when moving
  • Call us for anything you're unsure about

✗ Never Do

  • Use Harpic, Lizol, Colin, or harsh cleaners
  • Use lemon, vinegar, or acids on any material
  • Use scouring pads, steel wool, or hard brushes
  • Place hot diyas / camphor directly on any surface
  • Dry-wipe acrylic (always damp first)
  • Use acetone or thinner on DUCO
  • Leave water sitting on wood — ever
  • Apply polish or sealants without guidance

Seasonal Notes

Each season brings different care needs

🌧️

Monsoon

Ventilate the puja room. Use silica gel moisture absorber inside closed mandirs. Extra attention to wood and prelam edges.

☀️

Summer

Keep away from sunlight to prevent fading of wood, DUCO, and acrylic. Move mandir if direct sun hits it for more than an hour.

❄️

Winter

Diya ghee thickens in the cold. Refill carefully to prevent overflow stains — especially on marble and wood.

Moving Your Mandir

When in doubt, call us before you move it

Same Room

Easy Move

Empty completely, lift (never drag), minimum two adults. Place a blanket underneath if sliding on marble floor.

Between Rooms / Floors

Careful Move

Disassemble where possible, wrap each piece in bubble wrap + blanket. Clear the path first.

New Home or City

Call Us First
